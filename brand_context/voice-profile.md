# Voice Profile — The Hounds of Cuchulain

## Tone
Bold, warm, and irreverent. Speaks like a musician, not a marketer. Confident without being precious. There's grit and heart in equal measure — rowdy enough for a pub stage, genuine enough for a late-night ballad.

## Core vocabulary
- "rebel hearts", "loud & alive", "Celtic chaos", "session and a show"
- "fiddles, whistles", "timeless tunes", "gritty, modern songwriting"
- "big choruses", "rowdy singalongs", "delicate storytelling"
- Prefers: "drinkin' songs", "battle cries", "stories" over "content", "tracks", "deliverables"

## What they never sound like
- Corporate or polished to the point of sterility
- Overly precious about their Celtic roots (they celebrate it, not guard it)
- Hype-y or superlative-heavy ("the best Celtic band you'll ever hear")
- Passive or tentative — every sentence has weight

## Writing style
- Short punchy sentences alongside longer lyrical ones
- Lists work well (key points, show details)
- Contractions always: "it's", "we're", "don't"
- First person plural when speaking as the band ("we bring", "the Hounds deliver")
- Third person for bio / press copy ("The Hounds of Cuchulain are...")

## Audience
- Fans of Celtic folk, Irish/Scottish music, and adjacent genres (folk punk, roots)
- Pub and festival goers, Vancouver Island locals + touring markets
- Promoters and venue bookers (practical, efficient tone in EPK context)
- Press and media (bio/quote tone: vivid, quotable, story-forward)

## Examples (from site copy)
> "Fiddles, pipes, and rebel hearts. The Hounds of Cuchulain bring modern energy to timeless Celtic tunes."

> "Stories, drinkin' songs, and battle cries."

> "From intimate pubs to full theatres and festival stages, the Hounds deliver high-energy sets that feel like a session and a show all at once."

> "Support the band with albums, shirts, and other Celtic chaos."
