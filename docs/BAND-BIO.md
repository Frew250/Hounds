## Long bio (~220 words)

The Hounds of Cuchulain are a Celtic folk band based in Victoria, British Columbia — Celtic folk, loud and alive.

Rooted in the music of Ireland, Scotland and England, the Hounds take traditional reels, jigs and ballads and run them headlong into big choruses, rowdy singalongs, and gritty, modern songwriting. Fiddle and whistle trade lines over driving guitar, accordion and mandolin; a rowdy drinkin' song gives way to a delicate, late-night ballad without missing a beat.

They're equally at home in a packed pub, a full theatre, or a festival main stage. Their shows feel like a session and a show all at once — stories, drinkin' songs, and battle cries, played with grit and heart in equal measure, and a room full of people singing along by the end.

Whether it's a community Canada Day celebration, a nautical-themed festival, or a candlelit theatre, the Hounds bring the same thing every time: modern energy poured into timeless Celtic tunes.

Based on Vancouver Island and available for festivals, theatres, pubs, and private events across the region and beyond.
